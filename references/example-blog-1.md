# Example blog 1 — "What is a Mental Health Care Plan?" (The Empath GP, female voice)

Every week, I have a version of the same conversation. A patient comes in for something else — a repeat prescription, a referral, a skin check — and somewhere near the end, almost as an afterthought, they say: "I've actually been struggling a bit lately."
Almost as an afterthought. As if their mental health was a footnote.
If that sounds familiar, I want to tell you about something most Australians don't know they have access to: a Mental Health Care Plan.
What is a Mental Health Care Plan?
A Mental Health Care Plan (sometimes called an MHCP) is a Medicare-subsidised referral to a psychologist or other mental health professional. Your GP prepares it with you in a longer appointment, and it gives you access to up to 10 subsidised psychology sessions per calendar year.
That means professional mental health support at a significantly reduced cost — sometimes as little as $0 out of pocket, depending on the provider you see.
Who can get one?
You don't need to be in crisis. You don't need a diagnosis. If you've been feeling anxious, low, overwhelmed, or just not like yourself for a while, that's a legitimate reason to ask your GP about a Mental Health Care Plan.
Roughly 1 in 5 Australians experiences a mental health condition in any given year (Australian Bureau of Statistics). Most of them never access professional support. That gap isn't because people don't want help — it's usually because they don't know how to start, or they're not sure if what they're experiencing is "bad enough."
It is. You don't need to earn help.
What happens at the appointment?
Your GP will have a conversation with you about how you've been feeling, what's been going on, and what kind of support might help. It's not a test. There are no wrong answers. The appointment will be a bit longer than usual — we'll book you for a double slot to make sure we have enough time.
At the end of the appointment, if a Mental Health Care Plan is appropriate, your GP will write a referral to a psychologist or mental health professional and give you a list of providers. You take it from there.
Does it cost anything?
The GP appointment to prepare your Mental Health Care Plan can be bulk billed at Woodlands Family Practice — meaning no out-of-pocket cost. The psychology sessions themselves vary by provider. Many offer a gap fee of $20–40; some bulk bill entirely. Your GP can help you find a provider that works for your situation.
How do I book?
Just ask for a "mental health care plan appointment" when you call or book online. Let the receptionist know it's for a Mental Health Care Plan so we can book the right length of appointment.
If you're not sure whether you need one — book anyway. We can work it out together.
FAQ
Can I get a Mental Health Care Plan in Woodlands Perth?
Yes. Woodlands Family Practice offers Mental Health Care Plan appointments. Call us or book online and ask for a longer appointment for a mental health care plan.
How many psychology sessions does a Mental Health Care Plan cover?
Up to 10 individual sessions with a registered psychologist per calendar year under Medicare.
Do I need a referral to see a psychologist in Australia?
You don't need a referral to see a psychologist privately, but you do need a Mental Health Care Plan from your GP to access the Medicare rebate.
What if I've had a Mental Health Care Plan before?
You can renew it each calendar year. Your GP will review your progress and update the plan.
